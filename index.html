How to Play

Goal

Build a labyrinth that connects the exits with a single winding path.

Placing hedges

Each grid line between two dots has three states:

  • Undecided (thin gray line)
  • Hedge (thick green line)
  • Removed (no line)
A left-click places or removes a hedge. A right-click removes or returns an undecided line. You can also use Undo and Redo freely.

The clues

The number on a dot tells how many hedges meet at that dot. Use these to deduce where hedges must go and where they can't.

No square region will have exactly three hedges around it. A square with four hedges is fine—that's an enclosed area, like a tree, that the path goes around.

Winning

The puzzle is solved when the hedge walls form a single continuous walking path connecting the exits, with no loops or dead ends.

Why Daedalus' Labyrinth?

Daedalus was the legendary builder of the Labyrinth of Crete. Here, you're not Theseus trying to escape—you're Daedalus himself, shaping the maze one hedge at a time.
