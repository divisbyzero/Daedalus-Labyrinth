How to Play

Goal

Build a labyrinth that connects the exits with a single winding path.

Placing hedges

Each grid line between two dots has three states:

  • Undecided (thin line)
  • Hedge (thick line)
  • Removed (no line)
A left-click cycles forward: Undecided → Hedge → Removed → Undecided. A right-click cycles in reverse.

The clues

The number on a dot tells how many hedges meet at that dot. Use these to deduce where hedges must go.

A square with four hedges is allowed. It produces an enclosed area that the path goes around. (Imagine a large tree in the middle of the labyrinth.)

A red dot or a red square represents an error: too many hedges meeting at a dot or a square bounded by one or three hedges.

Winning

The puzzle is solved when the hedge walls form a single continuous walking path connecting the exits, with no loops or dead ends.

Why Daedalus' Labyrinth?

Daedalus was the legendary builder of the Labyrinth of Crete. Here, you're not Theseus trying to escape—you're Daedalus himself, shaping the maze one hedge at a time.
